Shadows of the Andes

Lead the 1780 rebellion against Spanish colonialism in this turn-based strategy game. Recruit soldiers, manage resources across Andean altitude zones, and liberate all 13 villages to victory.

Shadows of the Andes is an interactive turn-based strategy game that dramatizes the historical Túpac Amaru II rebellion against Spanish colonial rule in Peru. Rather than reading about history, you make history by commanding forces, managing logistics, and navigating the geographic constraints that shaped indigenous resistance in the 18th century.

The Story

Starting from Tinta, the historical birthplace of the rebellion, you play as Tupac Amaru II, leading a coalition of villagers and soldiers across the Andean highlands. Your mission is to occupy all 13 villages and expel colonial forces from the region. But this is no easy conquest. Resources are scarce, terrain is fragmented, and every decision has consequences.

Core Gameplay

Turn-Based Strategy: Each turn has two phases.

Your Phase: Command your army, recruit soldiers, move units, and gather resources.
Resolution Phase: Watch as resources are produced, battles resolve, and enemies move.

Altitude Matters: The Andes are not unified. High peaks, mid-valleys, and jungles each produce different resources.

High Altitude: Potatoes & Llamas, with fast travel support from llamas.
Mid Altitude: Corn & Quinoa, offering combat boosts and movement speed.
Low Altitude: Coca, used for healing.

Control villages across all zones to survive and thrive.

Tactical Combat: When your units clash with enemy forces, damage is calculated based on unit types, numbers, and resources. Female Corps units are defensive specialists, so use them wisely.

Resource Management: Feed your army. Starving soldiers are weak soldiers. Balance population growth, food production, and military expansion.

How To Play

Drag & Drop: Click a unit and drag it to a destination village to move.
Arrows: Navigate between four camera views of different regions.
Recruit: Click the Recruit button in player-controlled villages, costing 25 population.
End Turn: Click Next Turn to resolve the phase and advance.
Pause: Press ESC for menu options.

Camera Navigation: Arrows in the corners switch between Tinta, Andahuaylillas, Marcapata, and Jungle views. Plan your movements across fragmented terrain.

Unit Management: Select units from the left panel. Drag to other villages to command movement. Units locked in movement cannot be reassigned mid-journey. Stationed units generate resources and defend your territory.

Game Features

13 Historically Grounded Villages — Real locations from the 1780 rebellion.
Vertical Geography System — Altitude zones create real strategic depth.
Multi-Unit Combat — Tactical battles with dynamic damage calculation.
Resource Economy — Food production, consumption, and scarcity matter.
Tutorial System — Learn gameplay while understanding historical context.
Atmospheric Soundtrack — Immersive audio for exploration and conflict.
Victory & Defeat Conditions — Multiple paths to success or failure.

Victory And Defeat

Victory: Control all 13 villages.
Defeat: All your units are eliminated.

Design Philosophy

This game explores how geography, logistics, and resource constraints shaped indigenous resistance movements. Rather than glorifying war, it asks: What were the actual challenges Túpac Amaru II faced? By embedding these realities into mechanics, the game argues that interactive media is a powerful way to teach and understand history.

Tips For New Players

Don't rush early expansion — build up resources first, then push outward.
Watch your food supply — starving villagers produce less and your units weaken.
Use Female Corps defensively — their doubled attack power when stationed is invaluable.
Keep eyes on multiple regions — enemies can attack from any direction.
Plan long journeys ahead — moving between altitude zones takes many turns.
Recruit early, but strategically — more units means more hunger to manage.
