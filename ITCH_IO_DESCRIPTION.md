# Shadows of the Andes

## Short Description
Lead the 1780 rebellion against Spanish colonialism in this turn-based strategy game. Recruit soldiers, manage resources across Andean altitude zones, and liberate all 13 villages to victory.

---

## Long Description

**Shadows of the Andes** is an interactive turn-based strategy game that dramatizes the historical Túpac Amaru II rebellion against Spanish colonial rule in Peru. Rather than reading about history, you *make* history by commanding forces, managing logistics, and navigating the geographic constraints that shaped indigenous resistance in the 18th century.

### THE STORY

Starting from Tinta—the historical birthplace of the rebellion—you play as Tupac Amaru II, leading a coalition of villagers and soldiers across the Andean highlands. Your mission: occupy all 13 villages and expel colonial forces from the region. But this is no easy conquest. Resources are scarce, terrain is fragmented, and every decision has consequences.

### CORE GAMEPLAY

**Turn-Based Strategy**: Each turn has two phases:
- **Your Phase**: Command your army—recruit soldiers, move units, gather resources
- **Resolution Phase**: Watch as resources are produced, battles resolve, and enemies move

**Altitude Matters**: The Andes aren't unified. High peaks, mid-valleys, and jungles each produce different resources:
- **High Altitude**: Potatoes & Llamas (fast travel with llamas)
- **Mid Altitude**: Corn & Quinoa (combat boost & movement speed)
- **Low Altitude**: Coca (healing)

Control villages across all zones to survive and thrive.

**Tactical Combat**: When your units clash with enemy forces, damage is calculated based on unit types, numbers, and resources. Female Corps units are defensive specialists—use them wisely.

**Resource Management**: Feed your army. Starving soldiers are weak soldiers. Balance population growth, food production, and military expansion.

### HOW TO PLAY

**Controls**:
- **Drag & Drop**: Click a unit, drag it to a destination village to move
- **Arrows**: Navigate between four camera views of different regions
- **Recruit**: Click the "Recruit" button in player-controlled villages (costs 25 population)
- **End Turn**: Click "Next Turn" to resolve the phase and advance
- **Pause**: Press ESC for menu options

**Camera Navigation**: 
- Arrows in corners switch between Tinta, Andahuaylillas, Marcapata, and Jungle views
- Plan your movements across fragmented terrain

**Unit Management**:
- Select units from the left panel
- Drag to other villages to command movement
- Units locked in movement can't be reassigned mid-journey
- Stationed units generate resources and defend your territory

- **Victory**: Control all 13 villages
- **Defeat**: All your units are eliminated

### DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

This game explores how geography, logistics, and resource constraints shaped indigenous resistance movements. Rather than glorifying war, it asks: *What were the actual challenges Túpac Amaru II faced?* By embedding these realities into mechanics, the game argues that interactive media is a powerful way to teach and understand history.

## Tips for New Players

- **Don't rush early expansion** — Build up resources first, then push outward
- **Watch your food supply** — Starving villagers produce less and your units weaken
- **Use Female Corps defensively** — Their doubled attack power when stationed is invaluable
- **Keep eyes on multiple regions** — Enemies can attack from any direction
- **Plan long journeys ahead** — Moving between altitude zones takes many turns
- **Recruit early, but strategically** — More units = more hunger to manage
